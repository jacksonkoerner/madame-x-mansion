In 1859, a girl was born in this mansion who would grow up to become the most scandalous woman in Paris — and the subject of one of America’s most famous paintings.

Nearly two centuries later, her mansion still stands. Seven distinct residences in the heart of the French Quarter, where history lives in every room.

The Building

A three-story Creole masonry townhouse in the heart of the French Quarter, built between 1825 and 1830.

3 Stories

Historic Architecture

Porte-cochère entry, pilastered façade, wrought-iron balconies on the second and third floors overlooking Toulouse Street.

Private Courtyard

A lush courtyard with pond and water feature, gated parking for 5+ vehicles, and rear balconies overlooking the green space.

Fully Renovated

Complete renovation in 2012 with new slate roof in 2023. Updated electrical, plumbing, HVAC, and independent meters per unit.
